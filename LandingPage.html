GATE ECE Syllabus

The Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) for Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) covers a wide range of topics. The syllabus includes subjects such as Network Theory, Signals and Systems, Electronic Devices, Digital Circuits, Control Systems, Communications, Electromagnetic Fields, and more. Additionally, there are sections on General Aptitude and Engineering Mathematics. The exam assesses candidates' understanding of these core concepts in ECE and their ability to apply them to solve engineering problems. It is a comprehensive evaluation for admission to postgraduate programs and recruitment in related industries.

Syllabus

The ESE (Engineering Services Examination) and ECE (Electronics and Communication Engineering) syllabus covers a wide range of topics in electronics and communication engineering. It includes subjects like analog and digital electronics, signal processing, communication systems, electromagnetic theory, and control systems. Additionally, it encompasses general studies topics, including current affairs, ethics, and engineering aptitude. The examination is conducted in three stages: Preliminary, Mains, and Personality Test, with each stage focusing on different aspects of the syllabus to assess candidates' knowledge and expertise in the field.
